About me

About me

My name is Cynthia Umaña, I’m Colombian, mother of two amazing children, 9 and 6 years old and I once moved to the UK for the love of my life. But one day. His love was gone and my darkest moments of my life started. After living with deep depression for 11 years, where I was so lost, so empty, so unhappy, so alone, and at the same time, I created an eating disorder, where food was my escape, I was replacing that happiness that has to be inside us and created by us with sadness and an addiction to over eat to fill my empty space in my heart, a coping mechanism to forget about my pain. I was choosing to think and live in the past. In my past there was so much psychological abuse, rejections, insults and I was told how “useless” and “ugly” I was that I ended up believing it. And I forgot and abandoned the most important person who we need to love and cheer up and look after which is “ourselves”. I didn’t have any self-love and self-esteem.

It wasn’t until I experienced my most painful moment in my life. This break up. A separation from the person I was so in love with. But who was also hurting me. And I was left in a country where I wasn’t born. With two children. And no other family members to offer any emotional help or company. After my dramatic experience I realised that I was blaming my ex, it wasn’t him hurting me, I was hurting myself, to stay and attach myself to the past and to not take control of my thoughts and life and I was only feeling a victim of the world. My ex wasn’t a villain and him and me just needed to take separated ways to grow as individuals, as a person and mentally get healthier and to become wiser people who love and respect themselves. I learn to never depend on anyone in anyways and that the person I need to fall in love with and work on, it is myself. I learnt that happiness is the way how we choose to see life as it comes. With good things and bad things. And that we have to know how to appreciate when good things happen but also to learn to cope in healthy ways and still be happy while experiencing bad moments.

I learnt that our most painful stories of life are there to teach us a lesson, to make us wiser and strong. The past is a tool for as to use to create a better version of ourselves. I learnt that self-love, forgive myself and others and leave the past where it belongs, and choose very wisely my thoughts were the recipe for happiness. I am the owner of my thoughts, and if I choose to be happy, I will, if I choose to only focus in the present and work for my dreams I can reach and make anything possible. I learnt to look after my body, eat healthy and exercise for me. Not for my ex or to look pretty for the world or for any other men. But only for me, to please me and make myself happy and strong and have the best health and energy in the world for me and to be able to live the longest I can on this wonderful earth to be there for my kids to guide them and share the most amazing moments together. I’ve learnt that to be a great mother I needed to give them the best example of self-love and work on myself and show them that life is an amazing place to be where anything is possible.
